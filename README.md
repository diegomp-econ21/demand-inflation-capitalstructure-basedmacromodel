# demand-inflation-capitalstructure-basedmacromodel
Attempt at a Macro model which tries to reflect conventional Keynesian aggregate demand dynamics with an Austrian-inspired capital structure restriction and Phillips Curve. 

IS-FP-PC Macroeconomic Simulation Model (Keynes-Garrison-Phillips)

A dynamic, medium-term macroeconomic simulation model built in MATLAB. This project bridges the gap between demand-side keynesian macroeconomics (Aggregate Demand multipliers) and supply-side structural theories (Garrison's Loanable Funds Market and Hayek's Capital Structure friction), regulated in the medium term by an expectations-augmented Phillips Curve.

Unlike standard IS-LM frameworks where the interest rate is exogenously fixed by a Central Bank rule, this model introduces an endogenous real interest rate cleared through the **Loanable Funds Market**, capturing structural macroeconomic trade-offs:

**Aggregate Demand (Keynes):** Short-term output $Y$ is demand-driven, determined by private consumption (dependent on disposable income), autonomous government spending $G_0$, net exports, and private investment.
**Loanable Funds & Capital Friction (Garrison/Hayek):** When output exceeds its natural potential $Y > Y_{nat}$, resource scarcity drives up the real interest rate $r$. This monetary tightening induces a structural *crowding-out* effect on private investment $I$, capturing the temporal reallocation of capital.
**Inflation Dynamics (Phillips Curve):** Price levels are updated dynamically. A positive output gap $Y > Y_{nat}$ accelerates the annual inflation rate $\pi$, which in turn updates the aggregate price index $P$.

Based on the dynamic paths generated by the model, the interaction between demand-side constraints and an expanding production frontier yields highly sophisticated macroeconomic phenomena:

**Structural Demand Ceiling (Keynesian Steady State):** 
During the first 10 periods, the effective GDP (blue line) expands efficiently, binding perfectly to the economy's physical production capacity (red dashed line). However, beyond period 10, effective GDP completely plateaus near \$1055. This showcases a clear **Keynesian demand shortage**: even though capital accumulation continuously shifts the production frontier (FPP) upward toward \$1400, structural demand lacks the multiplicate velocity to utilize this idle capacity.

**Capital Discipline via Loanable Funds:** 
The initial output spike causes immediate friction in the loanable funds market, driving the real interest rate from 4% up to an acute peak of 16%. This price signal successfully disciplines the market, inducing a temporary contraction in private capital expenditure down to \$110 before it stabilizes near \$129 as the interest rate settles at an 8.4% long-run equilibrium.

**Secular Stagnation and Chronic Deflationary Pressures:** 
Because production capacity outpaces effective demand, the economy develops an expanding, chronic **negative output gap** $Y_t < Y^{cap}_t$. According to the expectations-augmented Phillips Curve, this persistent slack and idle capital erode aggregate price power, causing inflation to plunge systematically into a -10% deflationary spiral by period 60.
